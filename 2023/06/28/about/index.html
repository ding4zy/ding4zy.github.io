About

李孜 Li Zi

李孜，新加披国立大学博士，腾讯研究院资深专家，前新加披国立大学商学院特聘研究员，前麻省理工学院SAP（School of Architecture and Planning) 客座讲师，曾作为专家向新加坡国家发展部（The Singapore Ministry of National Development, MND）和新加坡总理办公室（Prime Minister’s Office Singapore, PMO）提供政策咨询。
Li Zi, PhD of Nation University of Singapore, recently worked on researching and strategic consulting for the Tencent Group as a senior expert. He previously served as a visiting research fellow at the NUS Business School and a guest lecturer at the School of Architecture and Planning at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (SAP). Mr. Li has also provided policy advice to the Singapore Ministry of National Development (MND) and the Prime Minister’s Office Singapore (PMO) as an expert.

李孜在欧洲、中国、东南亚和美国的电子商务和数字化领域拥有广泛的专业经验。他在2014年提出通过“数字服务”弥补城乡公共与商业服务和基础设施的不足，促进就业，实现虚实空间一体“一元”化的“在线城镇化”范式，将现实与虚拟空间的设计推向了人人参与的经济与社会的普惠与共建层面。
Mr. Li has extensive professional experience in the field of ecommerce and digitalization in Europe, China, Southeastern Asia and the US. In 2014, He proposed a paradigm of “online urbanization” that integrates real and virtual spaces into a unified model, using “digital services” to bridge the gap in public and commercial services and infrastructure between urban and rural areas, promoting employment and achieving economic and social inclusiveness and collaboration through people’s participation in the design of physical and virtual spaces.

他作为Domus国际中文版客座编辑推出了“在线”系列专刊，他的专著《Online Urbanization: Online Services in China’s Rural Transformation》被SpringerLink评为联合国可持续发展目标（SDG）方面最被使用的文献之一，专著《农村电商崛起》成为2019 年两会热议议题，在2016年受《经济学⼈》（Economist）杂志中文总编专访。
He, as a guest editor of the Chinese edition of Domus International, launched the “Online” series of special issues. His monograph “Online Urbanization: Online Services in China’s Rural Transformation” was recognized by SpringerLink as one of the most used contributions towards the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). His book “The Rise of Rural E-commerce” became a hot topic during the 2019 “two sessions”, and he was interviewed by the Chinese editor-in-chief of The Economist in 2016.
